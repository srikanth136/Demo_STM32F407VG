
clock in MCU:
    A clock in a microcontroller (MCU) is the fundamental timing signal, like a heartbeat, that synchronizes all internal operations (CPU, peripherals, memory) ensuring they work in unison at specific speeds, generated by oscillators (internal/external crystal) and managed by a clock tree with dividers/multipliers (PLLs) to provide different frequencies for various tasks, with higher frequencies meaning faster performance but more power. 
Key Functions
Synchronization: Ensures all digital circuits and peripherals (Timers, ADC, UARTs) operate in perfect time.
Speed Control: Determines the processing speed; faster clocks (MHz/GHz) mean quicker execution, while slower clocks save power.
Timing Generation: Provides periodic pulses (square waves) for counting and event triggering.

Types of clocks:
   
  Three Different types of clock source can be drive the system clock(SYSCLK)

  HSI oscillators clock -> Crystal oscillator(Interior  to microcontroller)
  HSE Oscillators clock -> RC Oscillator (Exterior  to microcontroller)
  Main PLL clock -> Phase Locked Loop (Internal to MCU)
